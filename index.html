Solarsystem

The solar system is a vast celestial system that consists of the Sun, its planetary companions, and various other objects such as moons, asteroids, comets, and dust and gas particles, all bound together by the force of gravity. It is the system that includes our own planetary home, Earth, as well as the Sun and all the objects that orbit it. The central and dominant component of the solar system is the Sun, a massive star that provides heat and light to the entire system. Orbiting around the Sun are a collection of objects, with the eight major planets, including Earth, forming the primary group. These planets, in order of their distance from the Sun, are Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. Each of these planets has its own unique characteristics, composition, and orbit.
